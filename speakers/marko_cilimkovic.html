Marko Ćilimković

Talk: Lessons From Escaping the Dependency Upgrade Maze

Software Developer at Infinum

Lessons From Escaping the Dependency Upgrade Maze

Open-source dependencies are double-edged swords: convenient when applying, dangerous if left unattended. To learn how we got all our Ruby on Rails projects updated with minimum time loss, what to do to keep a healthy app ecosystem, or how to pitch this investment to the clients - join our talk.

Open-source dependencies are double-edged swords: convenient when applying, but dangerous if left unattended.

In the last couple of years, we’ve performed maintenance of 20+ apps with severely outdated dependencies, and it’s safe to say we’ve learnt a few lessons along the 500 hours we put into it.

Join the talk to learn - the fresh approach to updating dependencies that will help you stay ahead of the curve - what does one team have to do in order to keep a healthy app ecosystem - and how to pitch this investment to the clients.

Bio

I’m Marko, though most people know me as Ćilim. It’s pronounced like “chillin’,” but with an “m” at the end. I hail from the picturesque country of Croatia.

My journey with Ruby began in 2014, and it’s been a passionate affair ever since. I co-founded a company specializing in web solutions built on Ruby on Rails, and since 2016, I’ve been a part of the Infinum Agency. Starting as a junior developer, I steadily progressed to a leadership role. However, I recently realized that my true passion lies in diving deep into code rather than managing teams, prompting my transition back to a senior engineer position.

During my downtime, you’ll often find me immersed in fantasy literature, playing console games, watching Anime, or exploring the intricacies of the Japanese language.

  • Favorite console game: Sekiro
  • Favorite Anime series: Hunter x Hunter
